G Grabber

Takes energy from systems. Zero-sum by instinct. Plays to win at others' expense.

Finite game · Zero-sum · The wolf
Characteristics
Every interaction framed as a transaction with a winner.
Charm deployed as tool, not expression.
Scarcity as worldview, not circumstance.
Extracts status, credit, attention, resources.
Rationalization is their primary cognitive tool.
Benefits / risks
Short-term: fast, visible results.
Tactical clarity — knows exactly what they want.
Depletes ecosystems and poisons trust.
Easers enable them; Aligners freeze them out.
Long-term: systemic collapse or lonely exile.
Situational example
Steers credit toward themselves in real time. In friendship, calls only when they need something — framed as catching up. When confronted: genuine confusion, then victimhood, then reframe. Never the author of their own harm.
A Aligner

Matches energy given. Logical reciprocity. Fair, firm, hard to exploit.

Conditional game · Tit-for-tat · The mirror
Characteristics
Reciprocity as operating principle, not just tactic.
Empathy functions analytically — reads rooms precisely.
Generous by default, switches off cleanly when exploited.
Tracks fairness without making it a drama.
Can forgive once — but remembers everything.
Benefits / risks
Stabilizing in functional systems.
Natural immune response to Grabbers.
Trustworthy precisely because conditional.
Can become mechanical — relationships as ledgers.
Misread as cold when merely consistent.
Situational example
Gives honest feedback, expects the same. Lends support generously the first time, attentively the second, measuredly the third. Names a dynamic once, clearly — then adjusts behavior without ceremony. Doesn't punish. Just stops.
M Multiplier

Creates energy. Infinite game player. Believes 1+1=3 and acts accordingly.

Infinite game · Abundance · The gardener
Characteristics
1+1=3 as genuine belief, not motivational poster.
Invests in others' potential without being asked.
Trust as a generative, renewable resource.
Sees systems, ecosystems, long arcs.
Gets genuine pleasure from others' growth.
Benefits / risks
Creates compounding value across communities.
Leaves institutions genuinely better.
Vulnerable to Grabbers without Aligner instincts.
Can be idealistic past what systems can bear.
Boundaryless generosity becomes enabling.
Situational example
Introduces two people with zero agenda. Mentors and takes deep pleasure watching others surpass them. Builds cultures that outlast their presence. Looks naive until you map the network they built over a decade.
E Easer

Gives energy away. Self-erasing. The Grabber's unwitting accomplice.

Self-negating · sheeple · The martyr
Characteristics
Gives without accounting — often without awareness.
Conflict-averse to a self-erasing degree.
Kindness as identity, not merely a value.
Absorbs tension so others don't have to feel it.
Confuses presence with disappearance.
Benefits / risks
Smooths friction, holds groups together short-term.
Often deeply loved — but not deeply known.
Primary operational enabler of Grabbers.
Repressed resentment surfaces as collapse.
Their absence, when it comes, shocks everyone.
Situational example
Apologizes for the thing done to them. Stays long past the point of health because leaving feels selfish. Becomes invisible glue nobody notices until it dissolves. When they finally collapse, everyone says: but they seemed so fine.
